Why Cryptography?

Cryptography has been used for thousands of years to help to provide confidential communications between mutually trusted parties. In its most basic form, two people, often denoted as Alice and Bob, have agreed on a particular secret key. At some later time, Alice may wish to send a secret message to Bob(or Bob might want to send a message) to Alice). The key is used to transform the original message(which is usually termed the plaintext) into a scrambled form that is unintelligeble to anyone who does not possess the key. This process is called encryption and the scrambled message is called ciphertext. When Bob receives the ciphertext, he can use the key to transform the cipher text back into the original plaintext; this is the decryption process. A cryptosystem constitutes a complete specification of the keys and how are they used to encrypt and decrypt. Let's see some classic and block ciphers!
